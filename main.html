ABOUT ME

I was born in France , spent my childhood in Africa and went to college in Paris. My dream had always been to become a journalist in the U.S., so I got a bilingual job at the United Nations in New York and eventually made my way to Columbia journalism school.

For many years afterwards, my world was all about journalism: I was a technology reporter at Reuters, then a news editor at Bloomberg in New York, breaking news on Samsung and editing stories about Vladimir Putin's billionaire friends.

But... ... ...

every year I found myself increasingly drawn to the "other side" of my industry: the technology that was powering the financial databases and news terminals .

So I rolled up my (mental) sleeves, stalked all kinds of tech meetups, and started down the road to coding .

The most important lesson: Learning to code is HARD -- and such a joy! Over the past few years I've learned JavaScript , including React.js, Node.js, Express.js; Git and GitHub ; HTML5 and CSS ; SQL and Python . So much to learn, so little time!
